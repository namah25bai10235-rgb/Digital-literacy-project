Part B – Prevention Checklist
Stay Safe Online (College Students – India)
Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols).
Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA) on email, banking, and social media apps.
Never share OTPs, PINs, or passwords with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank.
Verify links before clicking—avoid suspicious emails, messages, or unknown attachments.
Keep your apps and devices updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.
Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions like banking or payments.
Be careful on social media—don’t overshare personal details like phone number, address, or college ID.


Financial / UPI Safety Tips
Never approve unknown UPI collect requests—you should only enter your UPI PIN to pay, not to receive money.
Double-check UPI IDs and QR codes before making payments to avoid fraud.
Do not install unknown apps suggested by callers claiming to “help” with refunds or payments.
Reporting Cybercrime
Immediately report fraud at the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
Call the Cybercrime Helpline: 1930 for urgent financial fraud assistance.
